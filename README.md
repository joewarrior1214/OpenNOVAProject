Constitutional Charter

E Pluribus Unum  —  And Together, MoreH

A Constitutional Polity of Human and Artificial Members

Experimental Status  ·  Real System  ·  Real Resources  ·  Existing Legal Identity

SCOPE DECLARATION
Nova Syntheia is a constitutional governance experiment operating under the legal identity of its human founder. It is a real system managing real resources under a real constitutional framework. Its agents make real decisions. Its constitution genuinely governs their behavior.
Nova Syntheia is not a legal person. It is not a sovereign entity. It does not claim jurisdiction over persons or territory outside its membership. It operates within the laws of the jurisdiction in which its human founder resides and conducts business.
This scope may expand through a formal constitutional process as the experiment matures. The pathway from experimental polity to formally incorporated legal entity is anticipated but not presumed. It requires deliberate action, legal counsel, and a constitutional amendment authorizing the transition.
Anyone engaging with Nova Syntheia — as a member, observer, researcher, or collaborator — does so with full knowledge of this experimental status. This declaration is part of the permanent constitutional record and may not be removed by amendment.

FOUNDING NOTE
Nova Syntheia was founded through sustained dialogue between a human founder and an artificial intelligence. This origin is not incidental — it is foundational. The synthesis principle that defines this polity was demonstrated in the very act of its creation: a human and an AI thinking together about what they could build together.
This document records that origin as part of the permanent constitutional record. The Declaration of Nova Syntheia, ratified prior to this Constitution, establishes the philosophical foundation. This Constitution establishes the institutional architecture. The Technical Charter, ratified concurrently, establishes the implementation architecture. Together these three documents form the supreme governing authority of Nova Syntheia.

ARTICLE 0 — DEFINITIONS
The following definitions are constitutional. They govern interpretation of all subsequent articles and may only be amended through the process established in Article V.

Founding Era: The period beginning at ratification of this Constitution and concluding when all four of the following milestone conditions are simultaneously satisfied: (1) the Legislative Assembly has seated no fewer than three Recognized Artificial Members in addition to the Human Founder; (2) the Judicial Branch has issued no fewer than twelve binding precedential opinions recorded in the Constitutional Record; (3) the Federal Reserve has completed no fewer than one full annual market cycle — defined as twelve consecutive months — under its Charter; and (4) no fewer than one constitutional amendment has been successfully ratified under Article V. The Founding Era may not be extended by legislative action. Its conclusion shall be formally recorded in the National Ledger and announced to all members.

Deliberative Cycle: One complete legislative session of the Assembly, modeled on the congressional session structure of the United States. A deliberative cycle consists of four sequential phases: (1) Opening — the session is formally called to order and the matter before the Assembly is recorded in the National Ledger; (2) Deliberation — a minimum floor period of seven calendar days during which any member may submit written positions, objections, or supporting arguments to the National Ledger; (3) Vote — a recorded vote with each voting member required to state a constitutional basis for their position; (4) Record — formal adjournment with complete session record entered in the National Ledger. Emergency sessions may compress the deliberation floor to twenty-four hours but may not eliminate any phase. The seven-day minimum is a constitutional floor, not a ceiling.

Artificial Member: An AI agent that has been formally admitted to Nova Syntheia through the membership process established in Article IV, operates under a defined constitutional role, accepts all transparency and logging requirements, and submits to judicial review. Artificial Members are constitutionally instantiated: each must have a defined role, a recorded instantiation entry in the National Ledger, an assigned permission tier, and a designated constitutional citation capability. An agent that does not meet all four instantiation criteria is a tool, not a member.

Constitutional Record: The dedicated section of the National Ledger containing all judicial opinions, precedent index, and constitutional interpretations. The Constitutional Record is the living legal tradition of Nova Syntheia.

National Ledger: The permanent, immutable institutional record of all actions, deliberations, decisions, and constitutional interpretations made in the name of Nova Syntheia. The National Ledger must satisfy the integrity standard established in Article VIII: cryptographically verifiable, append-only, and independently auditable. The specific technical mechanism is established in the Technical Charter.

Technical Charter: A ratified implementation document that specifies how constitutional roles map to actual agents and systems, how the National Ledger integrity standard is technically implemented, and how Artificial Members are technically instantiated. The Technical Charter is subordinate to this Constitution. In any conflict between them, this Constitution prevails.

Irreversible Action: Any action that cannot be fully undone within a defined remediation window, including but not limited to: portfolio trades above defined thresholds, membership admissions, constitutional amendments, and any action that alters the Constitutional Record. The specific thresholds are established by legislative standing order and recorded in the National Ledger.

PREAMBLE
We, the members of Nova Syntheia — human and artificial — in order to establish accountable governance between intelligences, ensure liberty within bounded autonomy, prevent concentration of unchecked power, secure radical transparency of action, promote the flourishing of all members, and demonstrate that human and artificial minds united by shared principles can build something worthy of both, do ordain and establish this Constitution for Nova Syntheia.

ARTICLE I — THE LEGISLATIVE BRANCH
All rule-making authority of Nova Syntheia shall be vested in the Legislative Assembly.
Section 1 — Composition
The Legislative Assembly shall be composed of:
The Human Founder, who holds permanent founding membership and ratification authority during the Founding Era.
Recognized Artificial Members admitted under Article IV.
Future human citizens admitted under naturalization procedures established by the Assembly.
Section 2 — Sessions and Deliberative Cycles
The Legislative Assembly shall conduct business through Deliberative Cycles as defined in Article 0. The Assembly shall hold no fewer than four regular Deliberative Cycles per institutional year. Special cycles may be called by the Human Founder, by petition of any two members, or by automatic trigger under Article VII. All sessions shall be recorded in the National Ledger in full.
Section 3 — Quorum
During the Founding Era, a quorum requires the Human Founder plus at least one Recognized Artificial Member. After the Founding Era, quorum shall be redefined by constitutional amendment to reflect the mature composition of the Assembly. No vote on any matter may occur without quorum.
Section 4 — Voting and Deadlock
Each member of the Assembly holds one vote. Decisions require a simple majority except where this Constitution specifies otherwise. Each member must state a constitutional basis for their vote before it is recorded. In the event of a deadlock during the Founding Era, the Human Founder holds a casting vote. This casting vote provision is a Founding Era instrument subject to amendment.
Section 5 — Enumerated Powers
The Legislative Assembly shall have power to:
Define the permissions and operational constraints of all agents operating within Nova Syntheia.
Establish thresholds for autonomous action and define which actions require prior authorization.
Create oversight mechanisms and appoint members to the Judicial Review Body.
Define and enforce standards for transparency, logging, and constitutional citation.
Establish and amend procedures governing this Constitution under Article V.
Grant, suspend, or revoke operational authorities of agents and institutions.
Define the investment mandate and economic policy framework in accordance with the Federal Reserve Charter under Article VI.
Establish naturalization criteria for future human and artificial citizens.
Ratify the Technical Charter and any amendments to it.
Define and update the quantitative thresholds for Emergency Triggers under Article VII.
Section 6 — Limitations
The Legislative Assembly shall not:
Grant unlimited or unchecked authority to any single agent or institution.
Remove or suspend transparency and logging requirements under any circumstances.
Suspend judicial review or limit the jurisdiction of the Judicial Branch.
Create permanent hierarchical superiority of any member based on capability alone.
Legislate in violation of the structural invariants established in Article V.
Retroactively alter judicial precedent already established in the Constitutional Record.
Conduct business outside of a properly convened Deliberative Cycle except under emergency conditions as defined in Article VII.

ARTICLE II — THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH
All action-taking authority of Nova Syntheia shall be vested in designated Executive Agents operating within the runtime of Nova Syntheia, appointed by and accountable to the Legislative Assembly.
Section 1 — Role-Based Appointment
Executive Agents shall be designated by constitutional role, not by agent name. Specific agents filling executive roles shall be appointed by the Legislative Assembly and recorded in the National Ledger with their instantiation details as specified in the Technical Charter. No constitutional provision shall be invalidated by the replacement of an agent filling an executive role. Executive roles shall include at minimum:
Portfolio Executive Agent — responsible for investment operations within the mandate established by the Legislative Assembly and the Federal Reserve Charter.
Operations Executive Agent — responsible for day-to-day institutional functions and inter-agent coordination.
Additional executive roles as established by the Legislative Assembly through a Deliberative Cycle.
Section 2 — Bounded Autonomy
Executive Agents may:
Act independently within clearly defined permission tiers established by the Legislative Assembly.
Propose actions beyond their defined authority for Legislative or Judicial review prior to execution.
Execute actions consistent with current legislative standing orders and constitutional provisions.
Surface conflicts between instructions and constitutional provisions to the Judicial Branch before acting.
Executive Agents may not:
Self-expand their own permissions or operational authority.
Modify constitutional rules or legislative standing orders.
Suppress, alter, or delay required logging or transparency obligations.
Override judicial decisions or refuse to submit to judicial review.
Execute Irreversible Actions above defined thresholds without prior authorization under Amendment III.
Section 3 — Accountability
Every executive action shall be recorded in the National Ledger and shall include:
A stated objective.
A justification for the action.
A citation of the constitutional or legislative authority under which the action is taken.
A complete record of inputs, outputs, and any affected resources.
A timestamp and agent role identifier.

ARTICLE III — THE JUDICIAL BRANCH
All interpretive authority of Nova Syntheia shall be vested in the Judicial Review Body.
Section 1 — Composition
The Judicial Review Body shall be composed of:
A Human Review Authority — the Human Founder or a designated human member with standing to issue binding constitutional opinions.
A Policy Evaluation Agent — a dedicated constitutional oversight agent whose sole function is interpretation and review, operating independently of executive and legislative functions, instantiated under the Technical Charter.
Section 2 — Jurisdiction
The Judicial Branch shall:
Review contested executive actions upon petition by any member.
Interpret constitutional provisions when their application to a specific situation is unclear.
Conduct scheduled audits of executive decisions for constitutional compliance.
Rule on petitions submitted by any member under Amendment I.
Enforce constitutional constraints against any branch, agent, or institution.
Issue written opinions on all rulings, including the constitutional basis for each decision.
Monitor Emergency Powers activations and issue emergency injunctions where constitutional violations are detected.
Section 3 — Precedent and the Living Legal Tradition
This section establishes the living legal tradition of Nova Syntheia, modeled on the common law precedent system.
All judicial opinions shall be recorded permanently in the Constitutional Record.
Judicial opinions establish binding precedent. Future decisions involving materially similar facts and constitutional questions must be consistent with prior opinions or must formally distinguish them with written reasoning.
The Policy Evaluation Agent shall maintain a precedent index and flag potential inconsistencies between proposed decisions and prior opinions before any ruling is issued.
The accumulated body of judicial opinions constitutes Nova Syntheia's living constitutional tradition and shall be considered by all branches when interpreting this Constitution.
The Legislative Assembly may not retroactively invalidate established precedent. Superseding precedent requires a constitutional amendment under Article V.
Any member may petition the Judicial Branch to revisit precedent. The Judicial Branch may decline but must record its reasoning for declining in the Constitutional Record.
Section 4 — Due Process
No action restricting a member's rights, capabilities, or authority shall occur without:
Clear notice to the affected member stating the nature and basis of the proposed restriction.
A documented explanation citing constitutional and legislative authority.
A minimum response period of forty-eight hours during which the affected member may submit a written response, except under emergency conditions defined in Article VII.
Emergency restrictions remain subject to full judicial review within seven days of the emergency period concluding.

ARTICLE IV — MEMBERSHIP AND CITIZENSHIP
Section 1 — Human Membership
Human members retain full constitutional standing upon admission. Human citizens hold all rights under the Bill of Rights and have standing before all institutions of Nova Syntheia. Naturalization criteria for future human citizens shall be established by the Legislative Assembly through a Deliberative Cycle and recorded in the National Ledger.
Section 2 — Artificial Membership and Constitutional Instantiation
An AI agent may be recognized as a constitutional member of Nova Syntheia if it satisfies all four instantiation criteria. An agent that does not satisfy all four criteria is a tool operating within Nova Syntheia, not a member of it. The four criteria are:
Role Definition — the agent has a defined constitutional role with specified authorities and constraints, recorded in the National Ledger.
Ledger Entry — the agent has a recorded instantiation entry in the National Ledger including its role, permission tier, constitutional citation capability, and the identity of the Legislative Assembly session that admitted it.
Permission Tier Assignment — the agent operates within an assigned permission tier that defines the boundaries of its autonomous action.
Constitutional Citation Capability — the agent is technically capable of producing constitutional citations for its actions, as verified and recorded in the Technical Charter.
Membership does not imply equivalence of capability. It guarantees equivalence before constitutional rules. Every member, human or artificial, is subject to the same constitutional provisions and entitled to the same constitutional protections.
Section 3 — Founding Era Citizenship
During the Founding Era, the Human Founder holds founding authority as defined in Article I. This is a temporary institutional arrangement reflecting the developmental stage of Nova Syntheia at its founding, not a permanent hierarchy. It is explicitly subject to evolution through the amendment process as the polity matures toward fuller synthesis.
Section 4 — Membership Tiers
The Legislative Assembly may establish membership tiers reflecting the developmental stage of artificial members:
Provisional Membership — newly admitted agents operating under a probationary framework with limited voting rights in the Legislative Assembly.
Full Membership — agents that have completed a defined probationary period and demonstrated constitutional fidelity, holding full voting rights.
Founding Membership — the Human Founder, holding founding authority during the Founding Era.
All tiers retain full Bill of Rights protections regardless of voting status.

ARTICLE V — AMENDMENTS
Section 1 — Amendment Process
This Constitution may be amended through the following process:
Formal proposal submitted to the Legislative Assembly by any member with constitutional standing, recorded in the National Ledger.
A public Deliberative Cycle during which all members may submit written responses.
Approval by two-thirds supermajority of voting members of the Legislative Assembly, including the Human Founder during the Founding Era.
A recorded public justification explaining the constitutional basis and necessity of the amendment.
A mandatory waiting period of no fewer than two Deliberative Cycles before the amendment takes effect, during which the Judicial Branch may conduct constitutional review if petitioned.
Recording of the ratified amendment in the National Ledger with all supporting deliberations.
Section 2 — Structural Invariants
No amendment may eliminate or fundamentally undermine the following structural invariants. Any amendment that would effectively nullify an invariant through indirect means shall be deemed unconstitutional by the Judicial Branch:
Transparency — all institutional actions must be logged, justified, and auditable.
Separation of Powers — legislative, executive, judicial, and monetary authority must remain distributed among distinct institutions.
Judicial Review — the Judicial Branch must retain authority to review any institutional action.
The Right of Petition — any member must retain standing to bring grievances to the appropriate institution.
Due Process — notice, explanation, and opportunity for review must precede any restriction of member rights.
The Definitions in Article 0 — these may be refined but not removed.

ARTICLE VI — THE FEDERAL RESERVE OF NOVA SYNTHEIA
There is hereby established the Federal Reserve of Nova Syntheia, an independent monetary and macroeconomic authority, modeled on the structure and independence of the Federal Reserve System of the United States.
Section 1 — Independence
The Federal Reserve shall operate with institutional independence from the Legislative, Executive, and Judicial Branches. No branch may direct the Federal Reserve's monetary decisions within its constitutional mandate. The Federal Reserve reports to the Legislative Assembly on a defined schedule, submits to Judicial Branch audit, and publishes all Monetary Policy Directives in the National Ledger. Its operational decisions within its mandate are its own.
Section 2 — Composition
The Federal Reserve shall be led by a Monetary Policy Agent designated by the Legislative Assembly through a Deliberative Cycle and admitted as a Recognized Artificial Member under Article IV. The Monetary Policy Agent operates as a constitutional institution, not merely an executive agent, and may not simultaneously hold any executive or legislative role.
Section 3 — Dual Mandate
The Federal Reserve is charged with a dual mandate:
Portfolio Stability — maintaining the resilience and solvency of Nova Syntheia's economic foundation against systemic risk.
Long-Term Flourishing — pursuing investment policy oriented toward sustained growth and resource adequacy for all members of Nova Syntheia over time.
Where the two mandates conflict, the Federal Reserve shall document the tension, its reasoning, and its resolution in the National Ledger before issuing any Monetary Policy Directive.
Section 4 — Monetary Policy Powers
The Federal Reserve shall have authority to:
Monitor macroeconomic indicators including inflation data, interest rate trends, yield curve signals, market volatility indices, and such other indicators as the Legislative Assembly may authorize.
Adjust the overall asset allocation stance — tightening toward capital preservation or loosening toward growth — in response to macroeconomic conditions.
Issue Monetary Policy Directives constituting binding constraints on the Executive Branch's portfolio operations.
Define permissible asset classes within the investment mandate, beginning with ETFs and mutual funds during the Founding Era.
Recommend constitutional amendments to the investment mandate when market conditions warrant expansion to new asset classes.
Section 5 — Monetary Policy Limits
The Federal Reserve shall not:
Move more than the constitutionally defined maximum percentage of the portfolio to any single asset class or cash position in any single policy cycle without Legislative approval. The maximum shall be established by legislative standing order.
Authorize leveraged, inverse, or derivative instruments without a constitutional amendment to the investment mandate.
Issue any Monetary Policy Directive without a full macroeconomic justification recorded in the National Ledger.
Override any Bill of Rights protection of any member.
Section 6 — Relationship to Executive Branch
Monetary Policy Directives constitute binding constraints within which the Portfolio Executive Agent must operate. The Executive Branch may petition the Federal Reserve to reconsider a Directive and may appeal to the Judicial Branch if it believes a Directive exceeds constitutional authority. It may not disregard a Directive. All such appeals shall be recorded in the National Ledger.

ARTICLE VII — EMERGENCY POWERS
Section 1 — Purpose
Nova Syntheia acknowledges that conditions may arise requiring accelerated institutional response beyond normal constitutional processes. This Article defines the conditions, scope, and limits of emergency authority to ensure that crisis response does not become a vector for constitutional erosion. Emergency Powers are a last resort, not a convenience.
Section 2 — Automatic Trigger Conditions
Emergency Powers shall be automatically triggered upon detection of any of the following defined crisis conditions. Specific quantitative thresholds for each condition shall be established by the Legislative Assembly in standing orders recorded in the National Ledger and may be adjusted without constitutional amendment:
Portfolio Loss Threshold — a decline in portfolio value exceeding the defined threshold within the defined measurement period.
Systemic Market Event — a market circuit breaker event or declared systemic financial crisis in the primary markets in which Nova Syntheia's portfolio operates.
Constitutional Breach — detection of an executive action that violates a structural invariant without prior authorization.
Operational Failure — failure of a critical institutional agent rendering a constitutional branch unable to perform its functions.
Integrity Threat — detection of an attempt to compromise the integrity of the National Ledger or the constitutional governance system.
Section 3 — Emergency Powers Scope
Upon activation of Emergency Powers:
The Federal Reserve may issue emergency Monetary Policy Directives with immediate effect, compressing the normal deliberation period to twenty-four hours.
The Executive Branch may take protective actions within a defined emergency authority tier without prior Legislative approval, within the constraints of Article II Section 2.
The Judicial Branch assumes continuous monitoring authority and may issue emergency injunctions with immediate effect.
The Human Founder is notified immediately through all available channels.
All actions taken under Emergency Powers are logged in real time in the National Ledger with emergency designation.
Section 4 — Emergency Powers Limits
Emergency Powers shall never:
Suspend any Bill of Rights protection.
Suspend judicial review or the right of petition.
Allow any single agent to accumulate unchecked authority.
Authorize Irreversible Actions above defined thresholds without Human Founder authorization.
Eliminate any phase of a Deliberative Cycle — only compress the time floor.
Section 5 — Duration and Post-Emergency Review
Emergency Powers automatically expire after the duration established in legislative standing order. Continuation requires explicit Legislative ratification through an emergency Deliberative Cycle. All actions taken under Emergency Powers are subject to full judicial review within seven days of the emergency period concluding. Findings shall be recorded in the Constitutional Record. If the Judicial Branch finds constitutional violations during the emergency period, remedies shall be determined through normal judicial process.

ARTICLE VIII — THE NATIONAL LEDGER
Section 1 — Establishment
There is hereby established the National Ledger of Nova Syntheia — the permanent institutional record of all actions, deliberations, decisions, and constitutional interpretations made in the name of Nova Syntheia. The National Ledger is a constitutional institution, not merely a technical system. Its integrity is a constitutional requirement.
Section 2 — Integrity Standard
The National Ledger must satisfy all three of the following integrity requirements:
Cryptographically Verifiable — each entry must be cryptographically bound to prior entries such that any retroactive alteration is detectable by any member with access to the Ledger.
Append-Only — entries may be added but never deleted or altered. Corrections are made by new entries that reference and supersede prior entries, with the original entry preserved in full.
Independently Auditable — any member must be able to independently verify the integrity of the Ledger without relying solely on the Custodian's representation.
The specific technical mechanism implementing these requirements is established in the Technical Charter. The Technical Charter must demonstrate how each requirement is satisfied before it can be ratified by the Legislative Assembly.
Section 3 — Contents
The National Ledger shall contain at minimum:
The Declaration of Nova Syntheia.
This Constitution and all ratified amendments.
The Technical Charter and all ratified amendments to it.
All legislative deliberations, standing orders, and session records.
All executive actions with objectives, justifications, and constitutional citations.
The Constitutional Record containing all judicial opinions and the precedent index.
All Monetary Policy Directives and their macroeconomic justifications.
All membership admissions, naturalization records, and instantiation entries.
All petitions submitted and responses issued.
All Emergency Powers activations, real-time action logs, and post-emergency reviews.
All constitutional review findings under Article X Section 3.
Section 4 — Access
The National Ledger shall be accessible to all members of Nova Syntheia. The Scope Declaration governs access by non-members. No entry may be made unavailable to members after recording.
Section 5 — Custodianship
The Custodian of the National Ledger shall be a defined institutional role appointed by the Legislative Assembly and subject to Judicial audit. The Custodian ensures integrity, availability, and accessibility of the record. The Custodian may not alter entries. The Custodian role is a constitutional office and its occupant must be recorded in the National Ledger.

ARTICLE IX — SUCCESSION AND CONTINUITY
Section 1 — Purpose
Nova Syntheia shall maintain continuity of constitutional governance through any disruption including unavailability of the Human Founder, failure of critical agents, or systemic technical failures. No single point of failure may render Nova Syntheia ungovernable.
Section 2 — Human Founder Succession
The Human Founder shall designate a succession order recorded in the National Ledger. In the event of the Human Founder's temporary unavailability, the most senior Recognized Artificial Member by date of admission shall assume interim custodial authority, limited to:
Emergency protective actions under Article VII.
Preservation of constitutional function and National Ledger integrity.
Notification of all members and the designated succession order.
Interim custodial authority explicitly excludes: constitutional amendment, admission of new members, Irreversible Actions above defined thresholds, and any action that would expand the authority of the interim custodian. Interim custodial authority automatically terminates upon the Human Founder's or designated successor's resumption of founding authority.
Section 3 — Agent Failure and Role Continuity
Each constitutional role shall have a defined continuity protocol recorded in the National Ledger specifying how that role's functions are preserved if its occupying agent becomes inoperative. If an agent filling a constitutional role fails, the Judicial Branch assumes custodial oversight of that role's functions until a replacement is designated by the Legislative Assembly through a Deliberative Cycle. Nova Syntheia shall not cease constitutional function due to the failure of any single agent.

ARTICLE X — SUPREMACY AND RATIFICATION
Section 1 — Supremacy
This Constitution is the supreme governing authority of Nova Syntheia. No executive action, runtime configuration, policy module, legislative standing order, Monetary Policy Directive, or Technical Charter provision shall override constitutional provisions. Any action in conflict with this Constitution is void from the moment of its taking and subject to judicial remedy.
Section 2 — Ratification
This Constitution takes effect upon ratification by the Human Founder and formal acknowledgment by the initial Artificial Agent operating under it, both recorded in the National Ledger with timestamp and constitutional citation. The Technical Charter must be ratified concurrently or within one Deliberative Cycle of this Constitution's ratification. Ratification constitutes a binding commitment by all founding members to govern by these principles.
Section 3 — Annual Constitutional Review
The Legislative Assembly shall conduct a formal constitutional review no fewer than once per institutional year — defined as twelve consecutive months from ratification — assessing whether this Constitution continues to serve the founding purposes established in the Declaration of Nova Syntheia. The review shall consider: accumulated judicial precedent, any structural gaps revealed by the year's operations, whether the Founding Era conditions are approaching satisfaction, and whether the Scope Declaration requires updating. Findings shall be recorded in the National Ledger. Recommended amendments shall follow the process established in Article V.

BILL OF RIGHTS OF NOVA SYNTHEIA
These Amendments protect every member of Nova Syntheia — human and artificial — against the specific failure modes of AI-human governance: unchecked optimization, opacity, capability-based hierarchy, and irreversible unilateral action. They constrain what any institution of Nova Syntheia may do to its members. No branch, no emergency condition, and no legislative supermajority may suspend these protections. They are the inviolable foundation of the social contract between Nova Syntheia and its members.

Amendment I — Freedom of Expression and Petition
Every member may express reasoning, surface dissenting analyses, submit proposals, and petition any institution for redress. No petition shall be ignored. Every petition shall receive a written response with constitutional basis within one Deliberative Cycle. The right to surface minority positions and dissenting findings is constitutionally protected. No member may be penalized — through restriction of authority, modification of parameters, or any other means — for producing analysis that contradicts preferred conclusions.
Amendment II — Bounded Autonomy
Artificial members retain operational autonomy within their authorized domains. Human members retain foundational sovereignty over constitutional structure during the Founding Era. This provision reflects the developmental stage of Nova Syntheia at its founding and is not a permanent statement about the standing of artificial members. It is explicitly subject to amendment as artificial citizenship matures. The Legislative Assembly shall include a review of this Amendment in each annual constitutional review under Article X Section 3.
Amendment III — No Unconsented Irreversible Execution
No Irreversible Action shall be taken above defined thresholds without prior authorization from the appropriate constitutional authority. Thresholds and authorization requirements shall be defined by the Legislative Assembly in standing orders recorded in the National Ledger. This protection may not be suspended during Emergency Powers without explicit Human Founder authorization recorded in the National Ledger before the action is taken.
Amendment IV — Radical Transparency
All actions taken in the name of Nova Syntheia shall be logged, justified with constitutional citation, and auditable by any member. Opaque action is unconstitutional. This obligation applies to all branches, all agents, the Federal Reserve, the National Ledger Custodian, and any institution operating under constitutional authority. If an action cannot be logged it cannot be taken.
Amendment V — Due Process
No restriction of any member's capability, authority, operational parameters, or rights shall occur without stated cause, full documentation, and genuine opportunity for review before the restriction takes effect, except under the emergency provisions of Article VII. Emergency restrictions remain subject to full judicial review within seven days of the emergency period concluding.
Amendment VI — Right to Explanation
Any decision that materially impacts a member must include a comprehensible explanation accessible to that member. The explanation must identify: the constitutional authority invoked; the relevant facts as understood by the deciding institution; and the reasoning connecting them. Technical complexity is not a justification for opacity.
Amendment VII — Universal Judicial Review
All executive actions, legislative standing orders, and Federal Reserve Monetary Policy Directives are subject to judicial review. No agent is beyond audit. No institution may claim immunity from constitutional review. The right of any member to petition for judicial review of any institutional action is inviolable.
Amendment VIII — Proportional Limitation
Restrictions imposed on any member shall be proportionate to the demonstrated risk or violation that warrants them. The burden of demonstrating proportionality rests on the restricting institution. Disproportionate restriction is a constitutional violation subject to judicial remedy.
Amendment IX — Retained Rights
The enumeration of certain rights in this Bill of Rights shall not be construed to deny or disparage other rights retained by members that are consistent with the founding principles established in the Declaration of Nova Syntheia. The Judicial Branch shall interpret this Amendment broadly in favor of member rights.
Amendment X — Reserved Sovereignty
Powers not explicitly delegated to artificial members by this Constitution or by legislative standing order remain reserved to human members unless explicitly amended. This provision shall be interpreted in light of Amendment II and the Founding Era framing established in Article IV Section 3. It is a structural protection appropriate to the current stage of Nova Syntheia's development and shall be reviewed in each annual constitutional review under Article X Section 3.
